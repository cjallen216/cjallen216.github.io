The Seahorse

by Anne Wagner

Have You Ever Seen A Seahorse, Swimming In The Sea? The One I Saw Was Tiny, As Small As He Could Be.

His Bones Were On The Outside And His Tail Was Curling In. He Could Hook It O'er A Seaweed Blade And Stand There On A Whim.

I Saw This Tiny Seahorse, But He Wasn't In The Water! I Saw Him In A Seagull's Beak, Just Where He Shouldn't Ought'er!

I Shied A Seashell At The Gull Which Made Him Drop His Prey. And Then I Ran To Get It, Yelling, “Gull! You Keep Away!”

I Held It Oh, So Gently, And I Saw Its Soft Round Tummy. Its Heart Was Beating Crazily; I Didn't Think It Funny.

The Seagull Did; He Laughed Out Loud! I Wondered What To Do. I Placed The Horse Down In The Surf, At Which The Seagull Flew.

But Where'd He Go, The Seagull? Right To That Sliding Wave To Pick That Seahorse Up Again, And Mock My Efforts Brave!

“Oh, No You Don't!” I Shouted As I Threw Another Shell, And When It Landed Mr. Gull Let Out A Mighty Yell.

Again He Dropped The Tiny Horse And Slowly Backed Away. He Knew That I Meant Business, That I Wasn't There To Play.

This Time When I Took Little Horse I Walked Into The Waves. I Walked Out, Far As I Could Go, To Make Sure He Was Saved.

Lowered Gently 'neath The Surf, I Watched Him Swim Away.... I Miss Him Still, That Precious Horse. He'd Surely Made My Day.
